What is ELD and ELD Rule?

TXT ELD

The Electronic logging devices (ELDs) automate the records of commercial driving hours – Hours of Service (HOS) – to make sure drivers adhere to the legal limits, and it aims in reducing unsafe or fatigued driving. The ELDs can make the collection and reporting of driver hours easier, quicker without mistake.

The provision for ELDs was included with MAP-21 (Moving Ahead for Progress in the 21st Century Act), which was designed by the FMCSA to modernize transport systems and signed into law on July 6, 2012.

The ELD mandate rules were published in the United States in December 2015 by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA), and compliance was expected of all drivers using paper logbooks by December 18, 2017. Post the mandate the drivers started using Automatic Onboard Recording Devices (AOBRDs) for the purpose. However, the second phase of the term will take effect on December 17, 2019. By this time, data must be recorded directly from the vehicle's engine and automatically transmitted to the Department of Transportation (DOT). This means that all devices must be ELDs, and AOBRDs will no longer stay compliant to the mandate. The ELD mandate specified that commercial drivers needed to use one of the self-certified logging devices listed on the DOT website to record driving hours.

The vehicles equipped with TXT ELD will be compliant across all phases of the ELD mandate.

TXT ELD - FMCSA Certified

TXT ELD is FMCSA-certified ELD is connected directly to the vehicle for improved accuracy and automated logging. It simplifies compliance for your driver's legal obligations, helps reduce the paperwork, and encourage employee safety. Steps on how TXT ELD works?

  • 1
    The in-vehicle device embedded with mobile data terminal picks up real-time data on vehicle movement from the telematics units of vehicle ECM.
  • 2
    The drivers use the TXT application to update their duty status like on-duty, off-duty, driving, etc.
  • 3
    In both the above cases, the data flows to TXT I cloud on an integrated platform.
  • 4
    TXT flatform enables the data flow from both the scenarios to create a complete automated record of the vehicle movement log, which is compliant with the ELD regulation.
